✅ You can categorize countries into three types based on how power is distributed:**

---

## 🔹 1. **Unitary States**

* **Power is centralized** in the national government.
* Local authorities exist but only have powers **delegated** by the central government.
* The central government can **revoke or change** those powers.

🔸 **Examples**:

* **New Zealand**
* **France**
* **Japan**
* **Norway**
* **South Korea**

---

## 🔸 2. **Federal States**

* **Power is constitutionally divided** between the national government and subnational units (like states or provinces).
* Subnational units have **guaranteed autonomy** over certain areas (e.g., education, law enforcement).
* The central government **cannot revoke** this autonomy unilaterally.

🔸 **Examples**:

* **United States**
* **Australia**
* **India**
* **Germany**
* **Brazil**
* **Canada**

---

## 🔶 3. **Devolved / Quasi-Federal States**

* Technically **unitary**, but some regions have been **granted strong self-rule**.
* Devolution is often **asymmetrical** — different regions have different levels of power.
* The central government often **retains legal authority** to change or revoke devolved powers — but in practice, that's politically sensitive.

🔸 **Examples**:

* **United Kingdom** (Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland)
* **Spain** (Catalonia, Basque Country, etc.)
* **Italy** (special autonomous regions)
* **China** (Hong Kong & Macau – "one country, two systems")

---

## 📌 Summary Table:

| Type                       | Power Structure                   | Subnational Autonomy | Can Central Govt Revoke? | Examples                   |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| **Unitary**                | Centralized                       | Low to moderate      | ✅ Yes                    | New Zealand, France, Japan |
| **Federal**                | Constitutionally divided          | High                 | ❌ No                     | USA, Australia, India      |
| **Devolved/Quasi-Federal** | Technically unitary, but devolved | Moderate to high     | ⚠️ Yes (in theory)       | UK, Spain, Italy, China    |
